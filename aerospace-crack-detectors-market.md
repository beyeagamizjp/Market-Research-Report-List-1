<p><p>The aerospace crack detectors market features various technologies for detecting material flaws. Eddy current testing utilizes electromagnetic induction to identify surface and near-surface cracks in conductive materials. Dielectric testing measures changes in capacitance to detect defects in non-conductive materials. Imaging techniques, such as X-ray and ultrasound, provide visual representations of internal structures, revealing hidden cracks. Phased array technology employs multiple ultrasonic beams to create detailed images, allowing for precise detection of flaws in complex geometries, enhancing safety and reliability in aerospace components.</p></p>
