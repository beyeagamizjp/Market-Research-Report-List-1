<p><ul><li>Powder</li><li>Liquid</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The porcelain enamel market is categorized into two primary types: powder and liquid. Powder porcelain enamel consists of finely ground glass particles that are applied to surfaces and then fired to create a durable, glass-like finish. It is commonly used for appliances and cookware. Liquid porcelain enamel, on the other hand, is a paint-like formulation that can be brushed or sprayed onto surfaces, providing ease of application and versatile design options, often used in artistic and decorative applications.</p></p>
