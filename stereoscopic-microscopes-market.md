<p><p>Stereoscopic microscopes are available in two main types: binocular and trinocular. Binocular microscopes feature two eyepieces, allowing users to view samples with depth perception and enhanced detail, making them ideal for tasks requiring precision. Trinocular microscopes, on the other hand, include an additional eyepiece for a camera, enabling simultaneous viewing and imaging. This versatility makes trinocular models suitable for documentation and analysis in research and educational settings, expanding their utility beyond direct observation.</p></p>
