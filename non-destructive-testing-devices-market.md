<p><p>Non-destructive testing (NDT) devices encompass various techniques to evaluate material properties without causing damage. X-Ray testing uses radiation to reveal internal structures and flaws. Ultrasonic testing employs sound waves to detect defects and measure thickness. Magnetic particle testing identifies surface and near-surface flaws in ferromagnetic materials. Penetrant flaw detection reveals surface-breaking defects by applying a dye. Eddy current testing measures conductivity and identifies flaws in conductive materials. Other methods include thermal imaging and visual inspections, expanding the NDT market's applications.</p></p>
