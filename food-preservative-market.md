<p><p>The food preservative market is categorized into two main types: natural preservatives and chemical preservatives. Natural preservatives, derived from plant or animal sources, include substances like salt, vinegar, and essential oils that enhance shelf life while appealing to health-conscious consumers. In contrast, chemical preservatives are synthetic compounds, such as sulfites and nitrates, designed to inhibit microbial growth and extend freshness more effectively. Each type serves distinct consumer preferences, regulatory standards, and food safety requirements within the industry.</p></p>
