<p><p>Bioprosthesis valves, used in heart valve replacement surgeries, are derived from animal tissues. The two main types are porcine and bovine tissues. Porcine valves, made from pig heart tissues, offer flexibility and are often preferred for their natural function and ease of implantation. Bovine valves, sourced from cow pericardium, provide durability and are commonly used in younger patients due to their longer lifespan. Both types are designed to mimic natural valves, reducing the risk of rejection and enhancing patient outcomes.</p></p>
