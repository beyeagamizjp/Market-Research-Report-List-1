<p><p>The self-administered medication market includes various delivery methods for patients to manage their treatments independently. Oral medications are taken by mouth in forms like pills or liquids. Subcutaneous medications are injected under the skin, allowing for slow absorption. Nasal medications are administered through the nasal passages for rapid effects. Transdermal treatments use patches to release medication through the skin over time. Other methods may include inhalation or ocular delivery systems, catering to diverse patient needs and preferences.</p></p>
